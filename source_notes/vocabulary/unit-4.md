# Summary
Vocabulary and phrases used in job interviews, including preparation, confidence, first impressions, interview panels and job offers.

# Points
- Candidates may be shortlisted for interview based on selection criteria.
- It is important to do your homework before an interview.
- Body language and first impressions matter.
- Candidates may be asked about previous roles, responsibilities and notice period.
- A job offer may include salary, benefits and reporting structure.

# Examples
- I was shortlisted for an interview last week. | hiring process
- We believe your qualifications are an ideal fit for the role. | job offer language
- My notice period is two weeks. | employment terms

# Pitfalls
- Do not confuse a line manager with an interview panel.
- Do not use overly casual language when discussing experience in an interview.

# PracticePrompts
- Write five interview questions and model answers.
- Describe a previous role using formal interview language.
- Draft a short acceptance email for a job offer.
