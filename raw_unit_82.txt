A Homophones and homographs
Homophones are words with different meanings which are pronounced in the same way but are
spelt differently, e.g. row as in ‘at the end of the row’and roe [fish eggs].
Homographs are words which are written in the same way but have different meanings and may be
pronounced differently. Compare row in ‘we had a row /raʊ/ about who should pay for the tickets’
[argument] and ‘hesat at the end of the row’/rəʊ/ [line of seats].
